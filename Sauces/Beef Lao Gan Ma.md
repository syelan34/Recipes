## Ingredients
400g Fresh red chilis
1/2 tsp salt
80g Douchi (fermented black soybeans)
1.5 tbsp water
2 tsp whole Sichuan peppercorns
150g medium lean ground beef
1.5 tbsp fragrant red chili powder (eg kashmiri or gochugaru or Shaanxi Qin pepper)
#### Frying oil
1 cup oil (Preferably sichuan caiziyou or mustard seed oil)
8 cloves garlic, smashed
1 inch ginger, smashed
1/4 onion, sliced
#### Beef marinade
1/2 tsp salt
1/2 tsp Sichuan pepper powder
1/4 white pepper powder
1 tsp soy sauce
1 tsp Shiaoxing wine
#### Seasoning
1 tsp MSG
1 tsp sugar
1/2 tsp salt
1/2 tsp chicken bouillon powder
## Directions
Wash chilis and slice in half. De-rib and de-seed.
Place under sun in a strainer for ~30 min or until mostly dry. (Alternatively use salad spinner or paper towel).
Finely mince.

Rinse douchi, then soak in boiling water for ~30 mins, covered (must be <2 hours).

Toast Sichuan peppercorns for ~3 minutes over medium low heat, or until they begin to slightly lose color and leave small oil splotches in pan.
Grind into powder.

Prepare beef marinade and marinate.

To make the oil, first fry aromatics over a medium flame.
Once golden brown (~8 minutes), strain out aromatics.
Add oil back to pot.

Over medium-low flame, add the chilis.
Patiently fry chilis until they lose their moisture and are just starting to turn crimson.
You can increase heat to speed up the process but be sure not to scorch the peppers.
Add in chili powder and soaked douchi.
Fry for about a minute or two, or until the moisture in the douchi has evaporated.
Shut off heat.
Add in beef, sichuan peppercorn powder, and seasoning.
